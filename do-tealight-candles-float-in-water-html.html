Do all candles float in water, except from those mentioned above?

A floating candle is defined as a candle that, when submerged in water, displaces more water by weight than the candle weighs on its own. Therefore, when submerged in water, it floats! Not every candle, on the other hand, can float! These candles are often created in a spherical form, which enables them to float equally wherever they are put on a table or other surface.

Also, why do floating candles rise in the water as they burn, rather than sinking?

 The physical element is that the candle warms and expands the air around it. This momentarily prevents the loss of oxygen from occurring, and the water level remains low. When the oxygen supply is gone, the candle is extinguished and the air becomes cooler. The volume of the air decreases, while the volume of the water increases.
